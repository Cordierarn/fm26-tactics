## 1. DUAL-PHASE TACTICS
**The single biggest change in FM26.** Every tactic now has two completely separate formations:

- **In Possession (IP):** How your team moves and attacks when you have the ball
- **Out of Possession (OOP):** How your team defends when the opponent has the ball

**Key rules:**
- Same 11 players, but every player can have a DIFFERENT role in each phase
- Example: Left-back = Full-Back (Support) OOP → Wing-Back (Attack) IP
- Example: CM = Wide Central Midfielder (IP) → Wide-Covering CM (OOP)
- The game automatically switches formation based on possession

**Individual training now has 3 slots per player:**
1. IP role training
2. OOP role training
3. Additional attribute focus

**The 7-Step Tactic Building Process:**
1. Choose your IP formation (attacking blueprint)
2. Choose your OOP formation (can differ completely from IP)
3. Assign IP roles for each player
4. Assign OOP roles for each player
5. Set IP team instructions (passing, tempo, width, etc.)
6. Set OOP team instructions (pressing, traps)
7. Check the Tactical Visualizer — verify shape in both phases

## 2. BEST FORMATIONS & META TACTICS

### The Meta Landscape (Post Patch 26.3.0)
Formation still matters, but **roles and instructions matter more**. Wide positions are powerful — dribbling has been buffed significantly. Players who can beat their man 1v1 are more effective than in FM24.

### Top Formations by Playstyle

#### 4-2-3-1 (Most Versatile — Meta)
- Double pivot for defensive stability
- Central attacking midfielder for creativity
- Wide attacking outlets
- Creates "natural pressing triangles" and strong counter-press structure
- Best with: DM + CM pivot, AMC, wide forwards (not traditional wingers)
- Win rate: ~67% (still strong but no longer as dominant as FM22-24)
- Works for both dominant clubs and underdogs

#### 4-3-3 (Possession / Gegenpressing)
- Strongest for high-possession and high-press systems
- Elite winger utilization (half-spaces + wing overloads)
- Requires intelligent midfield balance
- Dual-phase version (attacks as 4-3-3, defends as 4-5-1) is among the best in the community
- Quadruple-winning version scored 120+ goals in testing

#### 3-4-3 / 3-5-2 (Defensive Cover + Aggression)
- Three centre-backs provide cover during transitions
- Wing-backs create wide overloads
- Numerically dominates midfield
- Requires: intelligent CBs, high-stamina wing-backs
- Effective for counter-attacking and controlled press

#### 4-2-4 (High Risk / High Reward)
- Statistically among the strongest formations post-patch (per FM-Arena data)
- Very high attacking output but defensive vulnerability
- Requires strong defensive midfielders and disciplined structure

#### 5-4-1 / 5-3-2 (Underdog / Low Block)
- OOP formation for protecting a lead or vs superior opposition
- Pair with fast IP counter-attack shape (4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1)
- Key: keep a CF high as a counter-attack target (Central Outlet CF or Splitting Outlet CF OOP)

### Underdog Blueprint
- **OOP:** 4-1-4-1 or 5-3-2 (Low Block)
- **IP:** 4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1 (fast counters)
- When needing a goal: switch OOP to Mid Block + push Wing-Backs to Advanced Wing-Back (IP)

## 4. DEEP DIVE: 4-3-3 FALSE NINE META TACTIC
*Source: FootballManagerBlog — tested with Newcastle in Premier League*

### Formation
- **IP Shape:** 4-3-3 → transitions to 3-3-4 in attack
- **OOP Shape:** 4-4-2 high block (Arsenal-inspired)
- **Concept:** Exploits numerical 3v2 midfield advantage vs 4-4-2 defenses

### Player Roles (Position by Position)

| Position | IP Role | OOP Role |
|----------|---------|---------|
| GK | Ball-Playing Sweeper Keeper | — |
| Right-Back | Inside Full-Back | — |
| CB (Right) | Standard Centre-Back | — |
| CB (Left) | Ball-Playing Centre-Back | — |
| Left-Back | Playmaking Wing-Back | — |
| DM | Defensive Midfielder (holds, stationary pivot) | — |
| CM (Right) | Wide Central Midfielder (drifts wide, carries) | Drops deeper |
| AM (Left) | Attacking Midfielder / Centre Forward (pins defense, half-spaces) | Moves to 4-4-2 shape |
| Right Winger | Inside Forward / Wide Midfielder (width, in-behind) | — |
| Left Winger | Winger / Wide Midfielder (stretches defense, takes on) | — |
| Striker | False Nine / Centre Forward (drops to overload midfield) | — |

### Team Instructions

**In Possession:**
- Build-Up: Short Pass (maintains staggered midfield)
- Style: Positive (through balls in-behind)
- Final Third: Patient (limits speculative shots)
- Transition: Ruthless counter-attacking

**Out of Possession:**
- High Block with aggressive pressing
- Forces opposition wide (invites crosses)
- Proactive pressing to regain quickly

### How it Creates Problems for the AI
1. Staggered midfield three pins opposition midfielders → creates spare defender
2. False Nine dropping → forces CBs into dilemma: step up (gap) or hold (midfield overloaded)
3. Wide rotations generate unmarked passing options
4. Front-four flexibility constantly tests the backline

### Results (Newcastle, Premier League)
- **38 games:** 30W 1L = 97 points (+16 clear of 2nd place)
- **Goals:** 105 scored (2.76/game), 18 conceded (0.47/game)
- **Clean sheets:** 21/38 matches
- **4+ goals:** 20 occasions across 65 games
- **Cups:** Won Carabao Cup, reached Champions League final

## 5. PLAYER ROLES GUIDE

### The Core Innovation: IP + OOP Roles
Every outfield player has two role selections. This lets you create exact hybrid profiles. Examples:
- Channel Midfielder (IP) + Box-to-Box Midfielder (OOP)
- Advanced Wing-Back (IP) + Holding Full-Back (OOP)

### New Roles in FM26

**Goalkeeper:**
- **Line-Holding Keeper:** Slightly higher positioning than traditional; shortens passing distances from defense without actively sweeping

**Defenders:**
- **Advanced Centre-Back:** Drops into midfield when pressure forms; retains defensive role while stepping into back-midfield to transition the ball
- **Wide Centre-Back (Support):** Steps into midfield progression lanes

**Wing-Backs / Full-Backs:**
- **Holding Full-Back:** Stays deeper when others press; gives flank coverage, sacrifices forward runs for structure
- **Pressing Wing-Back (NEW):** High-intensity wide presser winning ball in advanced positions; requires defensive cover to avoid exposure
- **Advanced Wing-Back:** More aggressive than traditional WB; stays high and wide as much as possible (primarily IP role)

**Midfield:**
- **Box-to-Box Playmaker:** Very effective in early saves; combines creative and dynamic movement
- **Wide Central Midfielder:** Drifts wide, carries, makes off-ball runs; drops deeper defensively

**Attack:**
- **False Nine:** Drops deep to overload midfield, dribbles in tight spaces, sharp passing
- **Inside Winger:** Hybrid winger role cutting inside aggressively
- **Wide Forward:** New hybrid role for wide areas

### Key Attribute Requirements by Role

| Role | Key Attributes |
|------|---------------|
| Gegenpressing (team) | Work Rate 14+, Stamina 14+, Teamwork 13+ |
| High Defensive Line | Pace 12+ (CBs) |
| Counter-Attack Forwards | Pace 15+, Acceleration 14+ |
| Sweeper Keeper | Rushing Out, Reflexes, Kicking |
| Ball-Playing CB | Passing 13+, Composure 13+ |
| Offside Trap | Anticipation 14+, Positioning 14+ |
| Short Passing Build-Up | Defender Passing 13+, Composure 13+ |

## 6. OVERPOWERED ROLES IN THE CURRENT MATCH ENGINE

### Tier 1: Dominant

**Shadow Striker (AMC position)**
- Called "the most overpowered role in the game"
- AI defensive marking often breaks down completely — the SS drifts unmarked
- Without a traditional striker, opposition CBs don't know who to mark
- Result: absurd goal tallies, late runs from AMC space untraceable by AI
- Best in: striker-less 4-2-3-1, 4-4-2 diamond, or 4-3-3 with no CF

**Inside Forward / Inside Winger**
- Dribbling buff in FM26 makes wide cuts highly effective
- When IFs cut inside aggressively, three players often attack central space simultaneously
- Difficult for keepers and defenders to manage
- Works best paired with overlapping WB/FB on the same flank

**Advanced Playmaker**
- "Can carry an entire tactic" — community consensus
- Operates with creative freedom in dangerous half-spaces
- Combine with Expressive creative freedom instruction for maximum effect

**Box-to-Box Playmaker**
- "Ridiculously effective in early saves"
- Combines creation and penetration in a single role

### Tier 2: Strong

**Wingers (Traditional)**
- "Wingers feel massively overpowered" — community verdict
- Dribbling engine buff = regular 1v1 wins vs full-backs
- Best deployed staying wide with IWB tucking inside (see Role Synergies)

**False Nine**
- Drops deep creating overloads
- Forces AI CBs into marking dilemmas they consistently fail to solve

## 7. ROLE SYNERGY — BEST PAIRINGS

### Defensive Pairings

**Advanced Centre-Back + Inside Full-Back**
- ACB "moves up to DM" (Expressive style) while IFBs "hold position" and "move inside to CB"
- Creates midfield overload + solid rest defence
- Best for: possession dominance with numerical superiority in central areas

**Half Back + Inside Wing-Back**
- HB drops between CBs (move back to CB, Careful style); IWBs tuck into DM positions
- Creates perfect 3-2 build-up structure
- "Absolutely controls and stabilises the build-up phase"
- Best for: beating high-pressing opponents

### Midfield Pairings

**Deep-Lying Playmaker + Defensive Midfielder**
- DLP: Expressive style, creative risk-taking, Hold Position
- DM: Careful style, positional discipline, Hold Position
- "Yin and yang" dynamic — creativity with defensive stability

**Box-to-Box Midfielder + Defensive Midfielder**
- BBM surges forward (Goal Threat, Moves Up to AMC)
- DM holds as insurance (stationary)
- "Stay and go mechanic" for dynamic midfield support

**Box-to-Box Midfielder + Inside Wing-Back**
- BBM surges forward while IWB fills vacated central space
- "Dynamic, unpredictable attacking movement without sacrificing balance"

### Wide Pairings

**Inside Wing-Back + Winger**
- IWB inverts centrally (Hold Position, Move Inside to DM)
- Winger stays wide (Stay Wider instruction)
- Forces defenders into lose-lose marking scenario
- Creates guaranteed midfield overloads + isolated winger 1v1s

**Wing-Back + Inside Forward / Inside Winger**
- WB overlaps down touchline (Get Further Forward, Stay Wide)
- IF/IW cuts inside as goal threat
- "Relentless overlaps" + "increased central threat"
- Best for: wide area overloads + forcing defensive dilemmas on opposition FBs

## 9. PRESSING & DEFENSIVE SETUP

### High Press / Gegenpressing Requirements
- **Mandatory:** Work Rate 14+, Stamina 14+, Teamwork 13+ (entire team)
- **Forwards:** Pace 14+
- **Centre-Backs:** Pace 13+ (to avoid being caught by balls in behind)
- **Trigger Press:** "More Often" rather than "Much More Often" unless fitness is elite
- **Counter-Press transition:** Gegenpressing within 5-6 seconds of losing possession

### Pressing Trigger Mechanics
- **ALWAYS:** Team holds shape until that specific opponent receives the ball, then swarms
- **NEVER:** Tactic-specific trigger points
- **No Instruction:** Default behavior based on team shape

### Pressing Triggers — When to Activate
- Opponent receives ball facing their own goal
- Slow back-passes
- Loose touch on the wing: winger/inside forward jumps first; full-back/wing-back arrives second
- Central pivot screens playmaker zone; far pivot slides to half-space

### Fatigue Warning Sign
- Monitor sprint distances — exceeding 11 km per player signals fatigue is compromising press efficiency

### Defensive Line Pairings (Critical)
| Line of Engagement | Defensive Line | Why |
|-------------------|----------------|-----|
| High Press | Higher / Much Higher | Keeps compact shape, enables offside trap |
| Mid Block | Standard | Balanced coverage |
| Low Block | Deeper | Avoids gaps in behind; protects against pace |

### Forming a Back Three
- **Half Back:** Drops between CBs when you have the ball → automatic back-three in build-up
- **Inside Full-Back:** Inverts inside + holds position → creates floating third CB in defensive moments

## 10. TACTICAL STYLE BLUEPRINTS

### Gegenpressing (High Intensity)
**Instructions:**
- Line of Engagement: High Press
- Defensive Line: Higher
- Trigger Press: More Often
- Defensive Transition: Counter-Press
- Tempo: Higher
- Passing Directness: Shorter (or Balanced)

**Squad requirements:** Work Rate 14+, Stamina 14+, Teamwork 13+, Forwards Pace 14+, CBs Pace 13+

### Counter-Attacking (Low Block)
**Instructions:**
- Line of Engagement: Low Block
- Defensive Line: Deeper
- Defensive Transition: Regroup
- Passing Directness: More Direct
- Attacking Transition: Counter-Attack
- Forwards: Pace 15+, Acceleration 14+

**Squad requirements:** Positioning 14+, Tackling 14+ (defenders); rapid forwards

### Possession Football (Tiki-Taka Style)
**Instructions:**
- Passing Directness: Shorter
- Tempo: Lower
- Build-Up: Play Through Press
- Patience: Work Ball Into Box
- Shots from Distance: Reduced
- Creative Freedom: More Expressive (only with high attributes)

**Squad requirements:** Defender Passing 13+, Composure 13+, team-wide technical quality

### 4-2-3-1 vs 3-4-3 Comparison

**4-2-3-1 Strengths:**
- Full-backs + double pivot = simple build-up outlets
- Natural 4-4-2 pressing shape (AMC pushes forward)
- Suits squads with strong AMC/playmaker
- Stable crossing patterns
- Recruit: vision-heavy AMC, crossing/stamina FB, off-ball IF

**3-4-3 Strengths:**
- Three CBs protect against transitions
- Wing-backs create wide overload patterns
- Man-oriented first-line press + half-space traps
- Recruit: mobile CB (pace + anticipation), dual WBs with crossing 12-15+, pressing CF with teamwork

## 11. CRITICAL INSTRUCTION CONFLICTS TO AVOID

| Conflict | Why It Fails |
|----------|-------------|
| Direct Passing + Work Ball Into Box | Contradictory patience levels |
| High Press + Deep Defensive Line | Creates massive gaps in behind |
| Counter-Press + Low Block + Regroup | Three conflicting defensive philosophies |
| Play Through Press + Long Goal Kicks | Strategy is immediately ignored |
| More Expressive + Low Attributes (under 14) | Constant turnovers, decision errors |
| High Tempo + Short Passing + Low Stamina | Players run out of energy quickly, tactic collapses |
| Offside Trap + Slow/Reactive CBs | Caught repeatedly by simple through balls |
| Too Many Attack Duties | Defensive transitions leave massive gaps |
| High Tempo + Short Passing (conflicting rhythm) | Contradictory pace vs. patience signals |

## 12. SET PIECES GUIDE

### New FM26 Set Piece System
FM26 has a dedicated **Set Piece Coach** staff role (has a "Set Pieces" attribute in their profile). Coaches display delivery style preferences and defensive formation preferences.

### Initial Setup — 6 Questions from Your Coach
1. Defensive marking style: Zonal / Hybrid / Man Marking
2. Counter-attack aggression from opponent set pieces
3. Delivery targets: Near post / Far post / Box center
4. Ball movement: Inswinging / Outswinging preference
5. Throw-in tactics by pitch area (defensive, middle, attacking thirds)
6. Penalty taker assignments

### Corner Routine Structure
Players are assigned to **role categories** (auto-updated after subs):
- **Aerial Threat:** Best headers of the ball
- **Box Threat:** Primary finishers
- **Recovery Defender:** Defensive coverage
- **Creators:** Playmaking support

The grey square indicator shows optimal placement — closer to it = better chance of scoring.

### Corner Tactics — Proven Approaches
- **Short corner:** Drag defenders out of position, create space for delivery
  - Use winger or full-back as short receiver
  - 3-4 players in near/far post zones
  - 1-2 players just outside area for cut-backs
- **Far-post delivery:** Beats zonal marking by targeting far post with aerial threat
- **Long throws (attacking third only):** Cause maximum chaos in the box; restrict to final third
- **Variety is key:** Rotate between near post, far post, and short — AI struggles to adapt

### Free Kicks
- **Direct (20-25 yards, good angle):** Usually the strongest option — assign best long-shot taker
- **Indirect:** Use when the angle is poor or distance too great
- **"Wall if needed" option:** Goalkeeper decides whether wall is necessary
- **Defensive line height:** Can be adjusted specifically for free kicks

### Set Piece Familiarity
New mechanic tracking player training on specific routines. More training = better execution consistency. Don't ignore set piece training sessions.

### Delegation Options
- Full delegation to Set Piece Coach: they manage training updates and personnel
- Full manual control: you design every routine yourself
- Hybrid: use coach for initial setup, refine manually

### Routine Packages (Available on FMScout)
The Hare's masterclass package includes:
- Attacking: Free Kick Deep, Free Kick Shooting Range, Free Kick Wide, Near Post Corner
- Defensive: Corner (Near Post Focus), Free Kick (Deep), Free Kick (Wide)
- Load ALL routines at once for proper compatibility

## 13. TRAINING GUIDE

### Core Principles

**Intensity Management (Critical):**
- Set every player to **Automatic Intensity** from Day 1
- Intensity should peak 2 days after matches, then taper before the next game
- Failing to set Automatic Intensity is the #1 cause of injury clusters

**Position-Specific Assignment:**
- Assign every player to a specific **Position / Role / Duty** (not generic position)
- This focuses development on attributes that matter for their actual responsibilities

### Seasonal Periodization
| Phase | Training Focus | Intensity |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| Pre-Season | High physical load + tactical work | Heavy |
| Normal weeks | Balanced mix | Medium |
| Fixture pile-up | Recovery emphasis | Light |
| Post-match (2 days after) | Peak physical sessions | Auto |

### Pre-Season Protocol
- Schedule friendly matches weekly at 45-60 minutes for starters
- Rotate to secondary teams for remaining minutes
- Use 6-8 weeks for tactical cohesion building before competitive matches

### Weekly Training Microcycle (4-2-3-1 / 3-4-3 Systems)
| Day | Focus | Activity |
|-----|-------|----------|
| Monday | Reset principles | Video debrief on press height and cover shadows |
| Tuesday | High press drills | Shape work: DM anchors; WB traps |
| Wednesday | Build-up vs pressure | GK/CB distribution, wing overload patterns |
| Thursday | Final third + rest-defense | Cutbacks, box entries, set-piece positioning |
| Friday | Match preparation | Pattern rehearsal, lock opposition instructions |
| Saturday | Matchday | — |
| Sunday | Recovery / rotation | Individual: stamina, decisions, positioning |

### Attribute Development Fundamentals
- Physical attributes: peak earliest, develop fastest early
- Mental attributes: grow slowest, need time and experience
- Technical attributes: steady growth, respond well to role-specific training

### Staff Impact
Quality coaches, sports scientists, and physios directly influence player progression. Fill ALL staff positions. Maintain balanced workload across training categories.

## 14. YOUTH DEVELOPMENT

### Age-Based Strategy

**Ages 15-17: Physical Foundation**
- Prioritize Physical Attributes
- Focus on positional training (defender / midfielder / attacker) NOT specific roles
- Players develop through training alone — match ratings not yet essential

**Ages 18-21: Technical Refinement**
- Focus on Technical Attribute training
- Assign preferred role for training focus
- Loan moves to appropriate competitive levels for consistent minutes
- Players aged 18+ progress faster through competitive matches (even 15 minutes counts)

**Ages 21+: First-Team Integration or Sell**
- Alternate between Mental and Technical Attribute focuses
- Incorporate desired Traits at this stage
- Either push for first-team placement or sell at peak value to reinvest in pipeline

### Youth Hack: Senior Squad Promotion
Moving 15-17 prospects (4-5 star potential) to the senior squad immediately grants access to:
- Superior first-team training facilities
- Better coaching staff
- Keep them available for U19 matches for game time

### Loan Strategy
- Set minimum playing time: **Regular Starter** or **Important Player**
- Reject Squad Player roles — bench time stunts development
- Verify the borrowing club has good training facilities
- Check for hidden mandatory purchase clauses (e.g., €18M after 25 appearances)

### FM26 Individual Training Slots (New)
Each player now has 3 training slots:
1. IP role training
2. OOP role training
3. Additional attribute focus (rotate every few months — physicals develop unevenly)

- Go to: Recruitment → Clauses
- Buy-out clauses = immediate budget boost
- Could make the difference in a key signing

**4. Backroom Staff Overhaul**
- Replace weak staff IMMEDIATELY (first action)
- Target highest star ratings in every coaching category
- Maintain Average or Light Coach Workload
- Maximize Physios and Scouts to full capacity

**5. Delegation Setup**
- Director of Football: Scouting reports, contract negotiations, transfer oversight
- Assistant Manager: Training schedules, tactical adjustments, youth development
- Head Coach: Match prep, team talks, substitution recommendations
- Medical Staff: Injury management, recovery protocols

**6. Code of Conduct**
- Establish strict discipline early
- Two-week wage fines for: missing training, straight reds
- Escalating to squad demotions for repeat offenses
- Prevents behavioral problems before they start

**7. League Rules & Quotas**
- Check: foreign player limits, home-grown quotas, U21 eligibility
- Build squad to match registration constraints

### Pre-Season Priorities
- Know transfer limits and salary caps before spending
- Fix needs before wants (backup striker > exciting winger if you have no depth)
- Target free agents and bargains (under €10M)
- Set evergreen recruitment focuses: wonderkids (15-21, 4+ star), expiring contracts, unhappy players
- Use Attribute Color Customization to highlight key stats per role

## 16. IN-GAME ADJUSTMENTS & DATA-DRIVEN MANAGEMENT

### What to Monitor Every Match
- Match momentum and xG trends
- Shot location maps
- Opposition overloads in specific zones
- Fatigue levels (sprint distance >11km per player = danger)

### Adaptation Scenarios

**If opponent dominates midfield:**
- Reduce tempo
- Add Support duties (remove Attack duties)
- Narrow defensive shape

**If opponent sits deep:**
- Increase tempo
- Add overlapping instructions
- Switch passing focus (one flank)
- Encourage dribbling

**If trailing with 20 minutes left:**
- Switch OOP to Mid Block (from Low Block)
- Push Wing-Backs to Advanced Wing-Back (IP)
- Add More Direct passing

**If protecting a lead:**
- Time Wasting: More Often
- Patience: Keep Ball in Play
- Reduce trigger press frequency

### Tactical Shouts
- **Praise:** After good spells or goals
- **Demand More:** When intensity drops
- **Calm Down:** Aggressive play or narrow leads
- Avoid spamming — use sparingly for maximum effect

### Data Hub Usage
Check over 10+ matches before adjusting tactics:
- Where shots come from (identify patterns vs. single results)
- How goals are conceded (systemic problems vs. individual errors)
- Pressing success rates vs. different formations

## 17. BEGINNER TIPS (TOP 10)

1. **Tame the UI with Bookmarks** — Create shortcuts to: Tactics, Squad Planner, Training, Recruitment Focuses, Scouting, Shortlist, Youth Squad, Data Hub

2. **Save Multiple Team Selections** — Store: Best XI, Rotation XI, High Press XI, Low Block XI

3. **Build Dual-Phase Tactics** — Always think in IP and OOP phases, not static formations. Match system to actual player profiles

4. **Fix Staff Responsibilities Early** — Delegate press conferences, tunnel interviews, friendly scheduling, and contract negotiations. Focus your time on what matters

5. **Maximize Recruitment Focuses** — Set evergreen searches: wonderkids (15-21), expiring contracts, bargain transfers. Customize attribute colors per role

6. **Periodize Training** — Pre-season heavy, in-season balanced, fixture pileup light. Let Auto Intensity manage match-to-match recovery

7. **Use Data Hub Before Adjusting** — Check 10+ game trends before changing tactics. Patterns matter; single results don't

8. **Use Shouts Strategically** — Praise after goals, Demand More when intensity drops, Calm Down with narrow leads. Don't spam

9. **Define Philosophy Before Recruiting** — Identify non-negotiable roles, find gaps, target players that fit your system

10. **Watch on Slow Speed When Testing** — Use 4x speed between highlights once systems stabilize; slow down fully when testing new tactics

### Additional Pro Tips
- Don't rotate in opening weeks — build confidence with your best XI first
- Spread money across multiple signings, not one expensive player
- Set up auto-save on Day 1 (Preferences → Auto-Save)
- Players aged 15-18 develop through training alone; 18+ need match time
- Even 15 minutes on the pitch counts as a match rating for older youth players
